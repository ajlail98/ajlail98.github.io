Projects

Sequencing viruses out of the air

My team is working on unbiased sequencing of all viruses in air. As a bridge to that ambitious goal, we've implemented Illumina's Viral Surveillance Panel v2 as a new component of our lab's existing school and healthcare air surveillance programs. Already we've made a few interesting observations, with more to come soon!

Retail Milk H5N1 Sequencing

During the 2024 outbreak of H5N1 in cattle, my team developed a tiled amplicon approach for sequencing viral material from retail milk. This sampling allowed us to monitor spread at the state level and to detect circulating variants in the absence of direct agricultural testing.

Science Magazine Article

SARS-CoV-2 Genomic Surveillance

My team and I were involved in several efforts to keep an eye on circulating SARS-CoV-2 lineages. I helped sequence Rapid Antigen Tests from library patients, a new model for opt-in community surveillance. My team also scaled up our sequencing capabilities to sequence several thousand retrospective nasal swabs in just a few months.

Plant Biotechnology

I validated parts of a plant genetics workflow to make macrochloroplasts, which would make chloroplastic genetic modification easier by presenting a much larger target for biolistic gene delivery.

Biosynthetic Compatibility

I executed in vitro assays using proteins from multiple bacterial biosynthetic pathways. Mixing a carrier protein from one pathway with a halogenase from another provides a greater understanding of the chemical constraints involved in PKS pathways and facilitates future bioengineering efforts using recombinant proteins from multiple species.
